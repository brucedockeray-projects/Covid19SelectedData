NHS Critical Care - were they prepared?

Lots of positive news stories concerning the new Nightingale Hospitals which are rapidly creating additional critical care capacity for the NHS. However it is worth reflecting that WITHOUT these measures the bed situation in the NHS was pretty bad. The current position is described in the recent Kings Fund Report (https://www.kingsfund.org.uk/publications/nhs-hospital-bed-numbers) dated 26th March 2020 which states that critical care beds (pre Corona Virus) were in TOTAl 5,900. It also reports that normal bed occupancy was 90 - 95%.

It doesn't take a genius to work out that using these figures would give a NATIONAL spare bed capacity of, at best, 590 critical care beds.

No wonder extreme measures have been needed.
