Care homes

We know that Care Homes, Hospices and the community are excluded from government daily data. The risk is we are understating the reach of this virus. There are around 11,0001 Care Homes in the UK which accomodate around 410,000 people. Today it has been disclosed that around 2,000 (approximatel 20%) Care Homes have Corona Virus infections.

So lets do some approximations - average care home number of residents 410,000 /11,000 = 37. Current total in infected homes = 37 x 2,000 = 74,000. Likelihood of catching it - say 40% = 74,000 x 40%= 30,000. Likelihood of survival say 70%. Expected deaths = 30,000 x 30% = 9,000.

And these estimates could be so much worse. Easily. The oldest,weakest, most vulnerable are at risk here. It is a time bomb.

Coronavirus deaths more than twice hospital toll, data indicate

In the week starting April 10, 22,351 deaths were registered in England and Wales, the highest figure since comparable weekly data started in 1993 and also worse than any figure in similar data of the past 50 years. The average for the comparable week from 2015-2019 was 10,497.

Since the beginning of March, there have been 27,015 more deaths registered up to April 17 than the five-year average for the time of year.
